Code of Conduct - Coimbatore Institute of Technology (CIT), Coimbatore

1. Identity and Campus Access:
Students must carry their institution-issued ID cards at all times and present them upon request by faculty, staff, or security personnel.
Loss of ID card must be reported immediately for replacement as per protocol.

2. Dress Code:
Boys: Formal pants with shirts and formal shoes are required. Jeans are prohibited during regular college hours.
Girls: Churidar with a long top and a dupatta is mandatory for a formal appearance.
Students must maintain a neat and presentable appearance at all times.

3. Class Hours and Schedule:
I Year students: 8:30 AM to 4:00 PM
II to Final Year students: 9:00 AM to 4:45 PM
Part-Time BE students: 6:00 PM to 9:00 PM
Late arrivals and early departures must be approved by the class advisor or concerned faculty.

4. Break Timings:
I Year: Morning break 10:20 AM to 10:35 AM; Lunch break 12:25 PM to 1:30 PM
II to Final Year: Morning break 10:50 AM to 11:05 AM; Lunch break 12:55 PM to 2:00 PM

5. Academic Integrity:
Cheating, plagiarism, or any form of malpractice will lead to strict disciplinary action, including suspension or expulsion.
Copying assignments, lab records, or project work from other students is prohibited.
Using unauthorized materials during examinations is a serious offense.

6. Prohibited Conduct:
Possession, use, or distribution of drugs, alcohol, tobacco, or any intoxicants is strictly forbidden on campus.
Ragging in any form is strictly prohibited and punishable by law as per UGC regulations.
Posting or sharing memes that target, accuse, or threaten any student, staff, or faculty member on social media is strictly prohibited.
Cyber-bullying, email theft, and sharing of private communications without consent are forbidden.
Bullying, harassment, discrimination, or any form of physical or verbal abuse is prohibited.
Any behavior amounting to sexual harassment or gender insensitivity will be addressed under the Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH) policy.

7. Campus Movement:
Loitering in corridors or common areas during class hours is strictly prohibited.
Students must be in their designated classrooms or labs during scheduled hours.
Permission from the class advisor is required to leave the campus during college hours.

8. Transportation and Parking:
Vehicles must be parked only in designated areas; unauthorized parking results in fines or towing.
Helmets must be worn by all students riding motorcycles or scooters.
Maximum two riders per bike; additional passengers are strictly forbidden.
Students must follow traffic rules within the campus premises.

9. Library Rules:
Students must remain quiet at all times inside the library.
All books and resources must be handled with care.
Damaging or defacing library property is strictly prohibited and will attract fines.
Late return of borrowed books incurs fines as per the library policy.
Food and beverages are not allowed inside the library.

10. Celebrations and Events:
Firecrackers, water balloons, or use of harmful substances (e.g., color powder) are banned on campus.
All student events must be organized with prior approval from the concerned authority.
Events must conclude by the stipulated time as approved by the administration.

11. Hostel Conduct:
Hostel residents must observe curfew hours, cleanliness, and noise control rules.
Cooking in hostel rooms is strictly prohibited.
Visitors are allowed only during designated visiting hours.
Students must not cause disturbance to other residents.

12. Property and Facilities:
Students must preserve institutional property. Any defacement results in fines and possible legal action.
Laboratory equipment, computers, furniture, and other college assets must be used responsibly.
Any damage to college property will be charged to the responsible student.

13. Faculty and Staff Interaction:
Respectful communication with faculty and administrative staff is mandatory.
Unruly behavior or insubordination will not be tolerated.
Students must follow instructions given by faculty during lectures, labs, and other academic activities.

14. Personal Belongings:
The institution is not liable for lost personal items.
Students are advised not to carry large sums of money or expensive items to campus.
Valuable items should be kept in secure locations.

15. Bus Conduct:
Students using college transport must remain seated during travel.
Loud behavior, creating disturbance, or disrespecting bus staff is prohibited.
Students must board and alight from the bus only at designated stops.

16. Anti-Ragging Policy:
Ragging in any form is a criminal offense under UGC regulations and the Tamil Nadu Prohibition of Ragging Act.
The Anti-Ragging Committee is chaired by the Principal, Dr. A. Rajeswari.
The committee includes a police officer, legal advisor, faculty members, and student representatives.
Anti-Ragging Squad conducts regular inspections of hostels, canteens, and common areas.
Helpline: +91 94883 16334
Email: principal.citoffice@cit.edu.in
Any student found guilty of ragging faces immediate expulsion and criminal prosecution.
