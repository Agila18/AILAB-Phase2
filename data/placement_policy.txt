Placement Process and Rules - Coimbatore Institute of Technology (CIT), Coimbatore

1. About the Placement Cell:
The Placement Cell at Coimbatore Institute of Technology is directed by a Professor-ranked staff member and is entrusted with the task of securing placements for graduating students. The cell maintains robust partnerships with national and multinational industrial establishments, facilitating pre-placement presentations, campus interviews, and recruitment activities. Student representatives from each department are selected to oversee and coordinate placement activities. Monthly review meetings are conducted for placement planning, and general-body meetings are held for final-year students at the start of each academic year.

2. Placement Infrastructure:
CIT provides state-of-the-art infrastructure for the placement process including dedicated rooms for group discussions, written tests, and interviews. A communication lab is available for aptitude and skill enhancement training. A placement library with aptitude and technical reference materials supports student preparation. Campus recruitment process information is displayed to students to aid career planning.

3. Training and Career Support:
The Placement Cell provides comprehensive pre-placement training to students aligned with industry trends, including:
- Aptitude and reasoning skills training
- Group discussion and personal interview preparation
- Technical skills enhancement workshops
- Communication and soft skills development through the communication lab
- Resume building and mock interview sessions
Pre-placement activities are coordinated for sophomore and junior students well before the final year.
The cell also supports students in preparing for competitive exams including GATE, TOEFL, GRE, CAT, and GMAT.
Self-employment promotion is also supported with guidance on government financial assistance for entrepreneurship.

4. Placement Process at CIT:
The campus recruitment process follows these stages:
Step 1 - Pre-Placement Talk (PPT): Companies visit campus and conduct presentations about their organization, job roles, work culture, and compensation packages.
Step 2 - Written Test / Online Assessment: Companies conduct aptitude tests, technical assessments, and coding challenges to shortlist candidates.
Step 3 - Group Discussion (GD): Shortlisted candidates participate in group discussions to assess communication, teamwork, and analytical thinking.
Step 4 - Technical Interview: Candidates are assessed on domain knowledge, problem-solving skills, and technical expertise relevant to the job role.
Step 5 - HR Interview: Final round to evaluate communication skills, personality, cultural fit, and salary expectations.
Step 6 - Offer Letter: Selected candidates receive official offer letters from the recruiting company.

Several reputed companies compete to be the "Day One" company each academic year, reflecting the strong demand for CIT graduates.

7. Placement Rules and Policies:
- Students must register with the Placement Cell to participate in campus recruitment drives.
- Professional dress code must be maintained during all placement-related activities.
- Students must carry their college ID card and updated resume copies during placement drives.
- Students must attend all scheduled placement activities (PPTs, tests, interviews) once registered. Failure to attend without prior notice may result in debarment from future placement drives.
- Students must maintain minimum required attendance and academic standing to be eligible for placements.
- Students must have completed all mandatory industrial training requirements.

8. Internship Programmes:
CIT facilitates internship opportunities with leading industries as part of the curriculum:
- B.E./B.Tech students: Four weeks of compulsory industrial training for eligibility for the degree award (mandatory since 2009).
- M.Sc. Integrated Programme students: Two 6-month internships during the 7th and 10th semesters in industries, institutions of eminence, and R&D laboratories.
- Internship companies include: EY, Justo Global, CaratLane, OptiSol, SPAN, Konnectify, Shaachi, Buckman, NGP.
- International internship opportunities available at universities abroad including UCF, ASU, UBC (Canada), NUS (Singapore), Ontario Tech, Athabasca University, and University of Malaya.
