Hostel Rules and Campus Life - Coimbatore Institute of Technology (CIT), Coimbatore

1. Campus Overview:
CIT is spread across a 69-acre campus located on Avinashi Road, Civil Aerodrome Post, Coimbatore-641014, Tamil Nadu. The campus is approximately 2.3 km from Coimbatore International Airport. The institution was established in 1956 by the V. Rangaswamy Naidu Educational Trust (VRET) and has been a government-aided autonomous institution affiliated to Anna University since 1987.

2. Hostel Facilities:
CIT provides on-campus hostel accommodation for both boys and girls.
The college has 3 hostels for girls and 2 hostels for boys.
There are separate hostels for first-year and final-year girl students to ensure a comfortable environment for freshers.

3. Room Configuration:
Two-sharing, three-sharing, and four-sharing rooms are available based on availability and preference.
In the first-year girls hostel, each room contains 4 chambers (4-sharing arrangement).
In the remaining hostels, rooms are generally two-sharing (2 students per room).
Each room is equipped with: a study table, bed, almirah (wardrobe), and ceiling fan for every student.
Washrooms and bathrooms are separated to maintain good hygiene standards.
Hostel rooms are spacious and clean with adequate ventilation.

4. Hostel Fees:
Hostel fees: Approximately Rs. 28,000 to Rs. 30,000 per annum.
Mess fees: Approximately Rs. 1,500 per month (subject to periodic revision).
Fees must be paid at the beginning of each academic year as specified by the hostel administration.

5. Hostel Amenities:
Free internet access (Wi-Fi) available for all hostellers.
24/7 ATM service available on campus.
Hygienic food served in the hostel mess.
CCTV surveillance installed inside the hostel premises for security.
Stationery shop available within the hostel area.
Gymnasium and fitness center accessible to all hostellers.
Basketball and badminton courts within the hostel premises.
Sports grounds for outdoor activities including cricket, football, hockey, tennis, and volleyball.
Cafeteria available for snacks and beverages outside mess hours.

6. Hostel Timings and Rules:
Hostel residents must observe curfew hours as specified by the hostel administration.
Students must register their entry and exit with the hostel warden.
Late entry requires prior permission from the warden.
Students must obtain written permission from the hostel warden before leaving the hostel for overnight stays.
Parents or guardians must provide consent for students to leave the hostel premises for weekends or holidays.
A leave register must be signed before departing and upon return to the hostel.

7. General Hostel Rules:
Students must maintain cleanliness and hygiene in their rooms and common areas.
Cooking inside hostel rooms is strictly prohibited.
Use of electrical appliances (heaters, irons, induction cooktops, etc.) in rooms is restricted. Students must use common facilities provided by the hostel.
Playing loud music or creating noise disturbances, especially during study hours, is not allowed.
Visitors must register at the hostel reception and are allowed only during designated visiting hours.
Students must not damage hostel property. Any damage to hostel furniture, fittings, or equipment will be charged to the responsible student.
Students must carry their hostel ID cards at all times within the hostel premises.

8. Mess and Dining:
The hostel mess provides breakfast, lunch, evening snacks, and dinner.
Meal timings are fixed and displayed in the mess hall. Students must follow mess timings.
Food will not be served outside designated hours except for medical emergencies.
Vegetarian and non-vegetarian options are available.
Feedback on food quality can be submitted to the mess committee.
The mess committee includes student representatives who work with the administration to maintain food quality.

9. Anti-Ragging and Discipline:
Ragging in any form is strictly prohibited inside the hostel premises and across the entire CIT campus. Ragging is a criminal offense under UGC regulations and the Tamil Nadu Prohibition of Ragging Act.
Any form of bullying, harassment, or intimidation will result in immediate disciplinary action, including expulsion from the hostel and potentially from the college.
Consumption of alcohol, smoking, and use of any prohibited substances is strictly banned in the hostel and on campus.
The Anti-Ragging Committee is chaired by the Principal, Dr. A. Rajeswari. The committee includes a police officer (Ms. Srilekha, Police Officer, Peelamedu), legal advisor (Mrs. N. Thangamani, Advocate), the Dean of Student Affairs (Dr. P. Vinayagam, Professor of Civil Engineering), and faculty members from various departments.
The Anti-Ragging Squad includes faculty heads and three student union leaders (Chairperson, Secretary, and Joint Secretary).
Anti-Ragging Helpline: +91 94883 16334
Email: principal.citoffice@cit.edu.in

10. Safety and Security:
Hostel premises are monitored round the clock by CCTV cameras.
Security personnel are posted at hostel entrances.
Fire safety equipment is installed in all hostel buildings.
Emergency contact numbers of the warden and security are displayed prominently in every hostel block.

11. Dress Code on Campus:
Boys: Formal pants with shirts and formal shoes are required during college hours. Jeans are prohibited.
Girls: Churidar with a long top and a dupatta is mandatory for a formal appearance.
Students must maintain a neat and presentable appearance at all times on campus.

19. Campus Life - Library:
The Central Library is spread across 13,881 sq. ft. and houses approximately 1,00,000 volumes including journals, books, and research project reports. It provides digital access to e-books and online journals through subscriptions. The library has a quiet study environment with dedicated reading halls. Students must remain quiet at all times inside the library, handle all books and resources with care, and return borrowed books on time to avoid fines.

20. Campus Life - Transport:
College bus service is available from various parts of Coimbatore city and surrounding areas.
Designated parking areas are provided for two-wheelers and four-wheelers.
Helmets must be worn by all students riding motorcycles or scooters on campus.
Maximum two riders per bike; additional passengers are strictly forbidden.
Students using college transport must remain seated during travel and maintain discipline.
