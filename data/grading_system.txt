================================================================================

Evaluation Method:
- Grades are awarded instead of marks

Purpose:
- Provides standardized and comprehensive assessment
- Ensures uniform evaluation across departments

Keywords: grade system CIT, marks vs grades CIT

================================================================================
SECTION: GRADE SCALE
================================================================================

Grade Distribution:

- S (Superior):
  - Grade Point: 10
  - Marks Range: 90–100%

- A (Excellent):
  - Grade Point: 9
  - Marks Range: 80–89%

- B (Very Good):
  - Grade Point: 8
  - Marks Range: 70–79%

- C (Good):
  - Grade Point: 7
  - Marks Range: 60–69%

- D (Average):
  - Grade Point: 6
  - Marks Range: 55–59%

- E (Satisfactory):
  - Grade Point: 5
  - Marks Range: 50–54%

- U (Unsatisfactory / Fail):
  - Grade Point: 0
  - Must reappear for exam

- FA (Fail due to Attendance):
  - Grade Point: 0
  - Must repeat course

- W (Withdrawal):
  - No grade points
  - Course withdrawn before deadline

- I (Incomplete):
  - Temporary grade
  - Must be cleared within time

Keywords: grade points CIT, grading scale CIT, S A B C grades

================================================================================
SECTION: SGPA CALCULATION
================================================================================

Definition:
- Semester Grade Point Average (SGPA)

Formula:
- SGPA = Sum of (Credit × Grade Point) / Total Credits

Expanded Formula:
- SGPA = (C1×G1 + C2×G2 + ... + Cn×Gn) / (C1 + C2 + ... + Cn)

Where:
- Ci = Credit of course i
- Gi = Grade point of course i

Keywords: SGPA formula CIT, semester GPA calculation

================================================================================
SECTION: CGPA CALCULATION
================================================================================

Definition:
- Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA)

Formula:
- CGPA = Total weighted grade points across all semesters / Total credits

Purpose:
- Measures overall academic performance

Keywords: CGPA CIT, cumulative GPA CIT

Keywords: internal marks CIT, continuous evaluation CIT

================================================================================
SECTION: CLASSIFICATION OF RESULTS
================================================================================

Based on CGPA:

- First Class with Distinction:
  - CGPA ≥ 8.5

- First Class:
  - CGPA: 6.5 – 8.49

- Second Class:
  - CGPA: 5.5 – 6.49

- Pass:
  - CGPA: 5.0 – 5.49

Keywords: class distinction CIT, CGPA classification CIT

================================================================================
SECTION: IMPROVEMENT OF GRADES
================================================================================

Rules:
- Students can reappear for end-semester exams

Purpose:
- Improve academic performance

Keywords: grade improvement CIT, reattempt exam CIT
