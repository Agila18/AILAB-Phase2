Examination Policy - Coimbatore Institute of Technology (CIT), Coimbatore

1. Examination Structure:
CIT conducts two types of examinations each semester:
- Two Mid-term Examinations (Internal Assessments) conducted during the semester
- One End Semester Examination (Term-end Examination)
The final result of the semester is evaluated by combining the mid-term examination results and the end-semester examination results.

2. Examination Schedule:
Odd Semester examinations are conducted in the month of November-December.
Even Semester examinations are conducted in the month of April-May.
The academic session begins with the Odd semester in August and ends in December, while the Even semester begins in January.
The examination timetable is uploaded on the CIT website prior to the commencement of examinations.

3. Question Paper Setting:
For Undergraduate (UG) programmes, all question papers are set by both external and internal examiners to maintain quality and fairness.
For Postgraduate (PG) programmes, the college has complete autonomy with internal question setting and valuation.

4. Valuation Process:
Answer scripts for UG programmes are evaluated by both internal and external examiners.
Students have the option to go through their valued answer scripts for transparency in the evaluation process.
If a student is not satisfied with the evaluation, they may apply for revaluation.

5. Passing Criteria:
Students must secure minimum marks in both internal assessment and end-semester examination separately to pass a course.
The passing grade is determined based on the combined performance in continuous assessment and end-semester examination.

6. Revaluation:
Students who are unsatisfied with their results may apply for revaluation of their end-semester answer scripts.
Revaluation requests must be submitted within the stipulated time frame announced by the Controller of Examinations.
A revaluation fee must be paid along with the application.

7. Supplementary Examinations:
Supplementary examinations are conducted for outgoing batches (final year students) who have arrear subjects.
Students who fail in any subject may appear for the arrear examination in subsequent semesters.

8. Continuous Internal Assessment:
Internal assessment includes mid-term tests, assignments, quizzes, seminars, and lab performance.
Continuous assessment in laboratory practicals, workshop practice, and engineering drawing has been part of the autonomous scheme since 2001.
Internal assessment marks contribute to the final grade along with end-semester examination marks.

9. Result Declaration:
CIT Coimbatore results for UG/PG courses are declared within 30-35 days after the examination.
The Odd semester examination result is declared in the month of January.
The Even semester examination result is announced in the month of June.
Results are published on the official CIT website.

10. Examination Misconduct:
Any form of malpractice during examinations (copying, use of unauthorized materials, impersonation) is strictly prohibited.
Students found guilty of malpractice will face severe penalties including cancellation of the examination, suspension, or expulsion depending on the severity of the offense.

11. Eligibility to Appear for Examinations:
Students must have a minimum of 75% attendance in each course to be eligible for the end-semester examination.
Students must have completed all required internal assessments and submitted all assignments.
Students must have paid all due fees and fines before appearing for examinations.

12. Mini Project Evaluation:
A mini project of 2 credits is offered to students in the V and VI semesters.
Mini projects are evaluated through presentations, reports, and viva voce by internal and external examiners.
