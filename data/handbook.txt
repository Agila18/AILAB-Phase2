Student Handbook - Coimbatore Institute of Technology (CIT), Coimbatore

1. About CIT:
Coimbatore Institute of Technology (CIT) was established in 1956 by the V. Rangaswamy Naidu Educational Trust (VRET). It is a government-aided autonomous engineering college located on Avinashi Road, Civil Aerodrome Post, Coimbatore-641014, Tamil Nadu, India. The college is affiliated to Anna University, Chennai, and is recognized by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). CIT has been an autonomous institution since 1987, granted by the University Grants Commission (UGC). The institution is accredited by the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) with 'A' Grade and by the National Board of Accreditation (NBA). CIT's motto is "Nature in the service of man."

2. History:
CIT was founded by Prof. R. Venkataswamy Naidu and Prof. P.R. Ramakrishnan in 1956. The college was initially affiliated with Madras University from 1956 to 1980, then moved to Bharathiar University, and later joined Anna University in 2001. CIT achieved autonomous status in 1987, making it one of the earliest autonomous engineering colleges in Tamil Nadu.

3. Campus:
The CIT campus spans 69 acres and is located near the Coimbatore International Airport (approximately 2.3 km away). The campus houses 14 departments offering undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs.

5. Governance Structure:
CIT operates through statutory bodies including:
- Governing Council
- Academic Council
- Finance Committee
- Board of Studies
Non-statutory bodies include the Planning and Evaluation Committee and Curriculum Development Cell.

6. General Rules:
All students must carry their ID cards at all times within the campus premises.
Smoking, alcohol consumption, and any form of substance abuse are strictly prohibited on campus.
Students are expected to dress appropriately and maintain proper decorum in classrooms and labs.
Mobile phones must be switched off during lectures and laboratory sessions.

7. Code of Conduct:
Any form of ragging, bullying, or harassment is a punishable offense and may result in immediate expulsion.
Vandalizing college property will lead to strict disciplinary actions including heavy fines.
Academic integrity is paramount; any form of plagiarism or cheating will face a zero-tolerance policy.
Students must maintain discipline and decorum during college events, symposiums, and cultural programs.

8. Academic Structure:
The academic year is divided into two regular semesters: Odd Semester (August to December) and Even Semester (January to May).
Each semester consists of teaching weeks followed by end-semester examinations.
CIT follows a Choice Based Credit System (CBCS) introduced in 2015.
Grades are awarded instead of marks for a more realistic and transparent evaluation system.
Students must consult with their academic advisors before registering for elective courses.

10. Programs Offered:
CIT offers a total of 34 programmes:
- 9 Undergraduate (UG) programmes (BE/BTech)
- 12 Postgraduate (PG) programmes (ME/MTech/MCA/MBA)
- 5-year Integrated M.Sc. degrees
- M.S. and Ph.D. research programmes
Total fees range from Rs. 80,410 to Rs. 7,40,000 depending on course level, structure, and duration.

11. Rankings:
According to the National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF) 2024, CIT is ranked in the 101-150 band in the engineering ranking.
